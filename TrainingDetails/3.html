Commisionerate of Afghan Refugee at GATTC

15 May, 2022

The Commissionerate of Afghan Refugees at the Ghulam Ahmad Technical Training Center (GATTC) aims to provide education and vocational training to Afghan refugees living in Pakistan. This initiative is a collaborative effort between the government of Pakistan and the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR). The Commisionerate provides a range of services to Afghan refugees, including language classes, computer courses, vocational training, and job placement assistance. The goal is to equip refugees with the skills and knowledge they need to become self-sufficient and contribute to the local economy. Language classes are offered in both Pashto and Urdu, allowing refugees to improve their communication skills and increase their chances of finding employment. Computer courses provide refugees with valuable IT skills that are in high demand in today's job market. Vocational training programs cover a variety of trades, including carpentry, plumbing, and electrician work. Upon completing their training, the Commisionerate offers job placement assistance to help refugees find work and become financially independent. The Commisionerate also provides guidance and support to refugees who wish to start their own businesses, promoting entrepreneurship and self-reliance. The GATTC is an ideal location for the Commisionerate of Afghan Refugees, as it offers state-of-the-art training facilities and experienced instructors who are committed to providing high-quality education and training. The Commisionerate serves as a beacon of hope for Afghan refugees who are seeking a brighter future, and it is a testament to the commitment of the Pakistani government and the UNHCR to support refugees and promote their well-being. In summary, the Commisionerate of Afghan Refugees at the GATTC is an important initiative that provides education and vocational training to Afghan refugees living in Pakistan. Through its various programs, the Commisionerate empowers refugees with the skills and knowledge they need to become self-sufficient and contribute to the local economy. It is a shining example of the positive impact that can be achieved when governments and international organizations work together to support refugees and promote their well-being.
